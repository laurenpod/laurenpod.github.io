Endangered And
At-Risk Birds
of Long Island

Saltmarsh Sparrow

Extremely rapid decline; highly specialized high-marsh breeder; sea-level rise is an existential threat. (NYSDEC / +1)

Roseate Tern (NE population)

Tiny, concentrated breeding population on LI islands; very sensitive to disturbance & prey shifts. (USFWS)

Piping Plover (Atlantic coast)

Federally threatened / NY endangered; beaches heavily used by people/pets. (NYSDEC)

Black Rail

Secretive marsh bird with very small, fragmented populations; vulnerability to marsh inundation. (NYSDEC / +1)

Clapper Rail (Ridgway’s / Clapper complex)

Tidal marsh specialist declining with marsh loss & flooding. (Audubon)

Red Knot

Long-distance migrant with steep population declines; relies on horseshoe crab eggs. (USFWS)

American Oystercatcher

Small breeding population on LI; vulnerable to disturbance & shoreline change. (Audubon)

Least Tern (coastal)

Nesting beaches limited and disturbed; locally vulnerable. (Audubon)

Common Tern

Nesting sites reduced; disturbance & predation issues. (Audubon)

Short-eared Owl

Grassland/shore-edge owl; severely reduced in NY and highly vulnerable on LI. (Grassland Bird Trust)

Northern Harrier

Open-country raptor declining due to habitat loss and wetland conversion.

Black Skimmer

Beach-nesting bird impacted by disturbance and reduced nesting sites.

Seaside Sparrow

Dependent on tidal marshes; vulnerable to sea-level rise and habitat loss.

Wood Thrush

Forest fragmentation and climate pressures drive declines.

American Bittern

Marsh specialist disappearing as wetlands vanish.

Black-crowned Night Heron

Breeding colonies shrinking on coastal islands.

Yellow-crowned Night Heron

Dependent on crabs and tidal wetlands; local losses recorded.

Great Black-backed Gull

Coastal changes and food source shifts threaten colonies.

Bald Eagle

Rebounded but still monitored on Long Island; lead poisoning and development remain issues.

Osprey

Recovered population, but vulnerable to contaminants and habitat loss.

Great Egret

Breeding colonies disturbed by human activity and rising seas.

Snowy Egret

Population unstable due to wetland changes and nesting competition.

Glossy Ibis

Saltmarsh and coastal habitat loss reducing foraging areas.

Tricolored Heron

Southern species at northern range edge; impacted by tidal habitat loss.

Willet

Coastal breeder; marsh flooding and development cause decline.

Black-bellied Plover

Migration staging areas shrinking due to beach disturbance.

Semipalmated Sandpiper

Shorebird populations dropping due to climate change and food scarcity.

Least Bittern

Smallest North American heron; losing breeding wetlands.

Eastern Meadowlark

Grassland bird disappearing due to mowing and farmland development.

Bobolink

Grassland songbird suffering from habitat conversion and pesticide use.

Horned Lark

Open-field bird declining due to loss of sandy, barren nesting areas.

Barn Swallow

Population declines linked to insect loss and habitat changes.

Tree Swallow

Cavity nester affected by competition and environmental toxins.

Purple Martin

Declines due to nesting competition and habitat changes.

American Kestrel

Small falcon facing sharp declines; nest box programs vital.

Merlin

Once rare, still limited breeder on Long Island; threatened by habitat change.

Northern Bobwhite

Nearly vanished due to loss of shrubland and fields.

Eastern Whip-poor-will

Nocturnal bird disappearing with insect declines and habitat loss.

Red-headed Woodpecker

Decline from forest management changes and loss of dead trees.

Eastern Wood-Pewee

Subtle but steady decline tied to forest structure and insects.

Chimney Swift

Nesting sites disappearing as chimneys are capped or removed.

Cedar Waxwing

Declines linked to climate shifts and fruiting plant loss.

Brown Thrasher

Shrubland songbird losing nesting areas to development.

Veery

Declining with forest fragmentation and climate changes.

Orchard Oriole

Rare nester on LI; loss of orchards and open woodland habitats.

Baltimore Oriole

Decline due to pesticide use and reduced insect populations.

Field Sparrow

Shrubland bird; population shrinking from suburban expansion.

Pine Warbler

Breeds in pine barrens; threatened by fire suppression and development.

Prairie Warbler

Dependent on early successional habitats; now rare on Long Island.

Black-throated Blue Warbler

Forest songbird facing decline from canopy loss and temperature changes.

Black Skimmer

Unusual seabird that skims water surfaces for fish. Threatened by beach disturbance and habitat loss. (NYSDEC)

Least Tern

New York’s smallest tern; nests in colonies on open beaches, vulnerable to recreation and predation. (USFWS)
